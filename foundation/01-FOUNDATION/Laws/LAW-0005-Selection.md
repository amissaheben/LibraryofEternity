# LAW-0005 — The Law of Selection

## Constitutional Definition

The constitutional principle by which one lawful expressive possibility becomes constitutionally actual without altering the identity from which the possibilities arise.

## Constitutional Maxim

> Nothing becomes actual unless possibility is constitutionally resolved.

## Ontological Function

Provides the irreducible constitutional capability of **Actualization**.

## Immediate Constitutional Consequence

**Actualization:** The unique capability contributed by this Law to the Minimal Capability Basis.

## Derived Constitutional Contributions

- Change + Selection → Historical Event
- Identity + Selection → Actualized Identity
- Selection + Resonance → Constitutional History

## Constitutional Invariants

- Selection cannot create possibility.
- Selection cannot alter identity.
- Selection cannot violate constitutional definition.
- Selection cannot guarantee persistence.

## Jurisdiction

This Law governs its named constitutional capability only. It must not be used as a substitute for any later measure, structure, threshold, interpretation, or institutional doctrine.
