# LAW-0001 — The Law of Identity

## Constitutional Definition

The invariant nature by which an entity is itself rather than another.

## Constitutional Maxim

> Nothing can become unless something first is.

## Ontological Function

Provides the irreducible constitutional capability of **Existence**.

## Immediate Constitutional Consequence

**Existence:** The unique capability contributed by this Law to the Minimal Capability Basis.

## Derived Constitutional Contributions

- Identity + Definition → Distinction
- Identity + Change → Energy
- Identity + Selection → Actualized Identity
- Identity + Resonance → Persistent Identity

## Constitutional Invariants

- Identity cannot emerge from another term.
- Identity cannot be replaced by another primitive.
- Identity cannot be dissolved through variation.
- Identity cannot be reduced to appearance or function.

## Jurisdiction

This Law governs its named constitutional capability only. It must not be used as a substitute for any later measure, structure, threshold, interpretation, or institutional doctrine.
