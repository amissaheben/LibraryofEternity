# LAW-0006 — The Law of Resonance

## Constitutional Definition

The constitutional principle by which the consequences of actualized participation persist as enduring conditions within the constitutional history of reality.

## Constitutional Maxim

> Nothing endures unless actuality leaves a constitutional trace.

## Ontological Function

Provides the irreducible constitutional capability of **Persistence**.

## Immediate Constitutional Consequence

**Persistence:** The unique capability contributed by this Law to the Minimal Capability Basis.

## Derived Constitutional Contributions

- Change + Resonance → Time
- Identity + Resonance → Persistent Identity
- Connection + Resonance → Persistent Relation
- Selection + Resonance → Constitutional History
- Resonance + Connection + Selection → Memory
- Resonance + Feedback → Learning

## Constitutional Invariants

- Resonance cannot create identity.
- Resonance cannot replace selection.
- Resonance cannot actualize possibility.
- Resonance cannot exist without prior actuality.
- Resonance preserves continuity without guaranteeing immutability.

## Jurisdiction

This Law governs its named constitutional capability only. It must not be used as a substitute for any later measure, structure, threshold, interpretation, or institutional doctrine.
