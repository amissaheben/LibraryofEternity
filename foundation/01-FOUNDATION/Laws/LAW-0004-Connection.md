# LAW-0004 — The Law of Connection

## Constitutional Definition

The constitutional principle by which distinct identities become capable of lawful participation in one another without loss of constitutional individuality.

## Constitutional Maxim

> Nothing can participate unless something can relate without ceasing to be itself.

## Ontological Function

Provides the irreducible constitutional capability of **Participation**.

## Immediate Constitutional Consequence

**Participation:** The unique capability contributed by this Law to the Minimal Capability Basis.

## Derived Constitutional Contributions

- Definition + Connection → Relational Space
- Change + Connection → Interaction
- Connection + Selection → Network Formation
- Connection + Resonance → Persistent Relation

## Constitutional Invariants

- Connection cannot eliminate identity.
- Connection cannot exist without distinction.
- Connection does not require similarity.
- Connection alone cannot produce organization.

## Jurisdiction

This Law governs its named constitutional capability only. It must not be used as a substitute for any later measure, structure, threshold, interpretation, or institutional doctrine.
