# AA-0003 — The Emergent Principles


## Prologue — The Nature of Measurement

Measurement does not create reality. It reveals ordered aspects of reality that already exist.

## Foundational Theorems

- **FT-0001:** Fundamental Laws govern existence; Emergent Principles quantify existence.
- **FT-0002:** Every Emergent Principle must derive from interacting constitutional elements, unless a single-law derivation is rigorously demonstrated.
- **FT-0003:** Ontological dependency shall never be interpreted as temporal sequence.

## The Four Primary Measures

| Principle | Constitutional Definition | Question |
|---|---|---|
| Time | The measurable ordering of unfolding and persistent state inclusion | When? |
| Space | The measurable organization of relational separation | Where? |
| Energy | The measurable capacity of an identity to undergo or produce transformation | How much transformation? |
| Information | The measurable organization of distinguishable reality | What pattern? |

## Jurisdiction

The Primary Measures quantify reality. They do not determine meaning, wisdom, justice, purpose, or consciousness.

## Archivist's Oath

The Archivist shall not mistake measurement for existence, certainty for understanding, or representation for reality. Every measure must remain within its constitutional jurisdiction.
