# AA-0005 — Life


## Prologue — The Living Question

Life is not a substance added to matter. It is the constitutional capability of a self-producing system to navigate its own persistence.

## The Vital Threshold

Autopoiesis establishes an enduring individual. Life begins when that individual acquires Intrinsic Preference: the constitutional asymmetry by which some futures preserve viability and others do not.

## Vital Foundations

### Intrinsic Preference
Life begins when the future is no longer constitutionally equal.

### Viability
Life does not preserve stability; it preserves the possibility of continued organization under changing conditions.

### Constitutional Expression
Core identity preserves continuity while expressive identity permits variation.

### Field of Variation
Life inherits a landscape of lawful expressive alternatives. Variation permits change; preference determines whether opportunity becomes history.

### Adaptation
Adaptation is the constitutional refinement of expression through repeated participation, selection, and resonance.

## Vital Testament

Life is distinct from consciousness, intelligence, and purpose. A living system may learn, adapt, preserve history, and negotiate viability without internally representing those processes as lived participation.

## Closing Maxim

> Life is not the awakening of matter. It is the awakening of participation.
