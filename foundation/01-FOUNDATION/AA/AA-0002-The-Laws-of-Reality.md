# AA-0002 — The Laws of Reality


## Purpose

To codify the six irreducible constitutional Laws governing every scale of reality.

## The Six Pillars

1. **Identity — Being:** Every entity possesses an intrinsic nature.
2. **Definition — Distinction:** Every identity possesses constitutional limits.
3. **Change — Expression:** Every identity may lawfully occupy different expressive states.
4. **Connection — Participation:** Distinct identities may enter lawful relation without losing individuality.
5. **Selection — Actualization:** One lawful expressive possibility becomes constitutionally actual.
6. **Resonance — Persistence:** Actualized participation leaves enduring constitutional consequence.

## Architectural Domain Split

### Intrinsic Constitution
Identity, Definition, and Change govern what an entity is within itself.

### Relational Constitution
Connection, Selection, and Resonance govern how an entity participates within reality.

## Minimal Capability Basis

| Law | Irreducible Capability |
|---|---|
| Identity | Existence |
| Definition | Boundedness |
| Change | Expression |
| Connection | Participation |
| Selection | Actualization |
| Resonance | Persistence |

## Constitutional Rule

All higher-order realities must be derived from lawful combinations of these six capabilities. No additional fundamental Law may be admitted unless it introduces a demonstrably irreducible capability.
